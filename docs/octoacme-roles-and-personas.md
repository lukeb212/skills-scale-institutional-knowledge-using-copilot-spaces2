# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises. It serves as the **single source of truth** for role definitions across all OctoAcme project documentation.

> **Note:** For role-related questions during projects, always consult this document first.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on Agile best practices. They ensure the team adheres to Scrum principles while enabling continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team progress
- Coach the team on Agile/Scrum practices and values
- Shield the team from external distractions during sprints
- Track sprint health and velocity metrics
- Promote continuous improvement and team self-organization

### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Improve sprint predictability and delivery cadence
- Foster a culture of transparency and continuous learning

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Impediment escalation to Project Manager or stakeholders
- Agile coaching conversations with team members

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager:** Aligns on process, escalates organizational impediments, and coordinates on timeline impacts
- **Product Manager:** Ensures backlog refinement and sprint goals are clear
- **Developers:** Supports during sprints, removes blockers, and facilitates ceremonies

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs, prototypes, and interfaces based on user research and business requirements. They ensure the product is intuitive, accessible, and meets user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Collaborate on design systems and component libraries
- Validate designs with users and iterate based on feedback
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-friendly experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Maintain consistency across product interfaces

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques
- User research findings presentations
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation
- Stakeholder presentations on design direction

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager:** Clarifies requirements, aligns on user personas, and validates design solutions
- **Developers:** Collaborates on implementation feasibility and handoff
- **Data Analyst:** Reviews user behavior data to inform design decisions

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts measure project outcomes, create dashboards, and provide actionable insights to inform decision-making. They help the team understand performance and user behavior through data.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track success metrics and KPIs
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Analyze user behavior and product performance data
- Provide data-driven recommendations for improvements
- Support A/B testing and experiment analysis
- Document data sources and methodology

### Goals
- Enable data-informed decision-making across the team
- Provide clear, actionable insights from complex data
- Measure and communicate project impact and outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews and dashboard updates
- Data findings presentations to stakeholders
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and reports
- Collaboration on experiment design and results

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager:** Defines success metrics and reviews performance data
- **Project Manager:** Provides project health metrics and outcome tracking
- **UX Designer:** Shares user behavior insights to inform design decisions

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers advocate for user needs post-launch, collect feedback, and drive product adoption. They serve as the bridge between customers and the product team.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback and feature requests
- Monitor customer satisfaction and adoption metrics
- Advocate for customer needs in planning and prioritization
- Coordinate with support teams on customer issues
- Drive product adoption and user onboarding improvements
- Communicate product updates and releases to customers

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Ensure customer voice is represented in product decisions
- Drive successful product adoption and engagement

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and reports
- Participation in planning sessions to represent customer needs
- Coordination with support and sales teams
- Customer communications on releases and updates

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager:** Loops customer insights into roadmap and prioritization
- **Project Manager:** Coordinates on customer-impacting timelines and releases
- **Data Analyst:** Reviews customer success metrics and adoption data

## Cross-functional Interaction Matrix

This matrix clarifies how all roles interact during major project phases.

| Phase | Project Manager | Product Manager | Developers | Scrum Master | UX Designer | Data Analyst | Customer Success Manager |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|--------------|-------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Initiation** | Leads kickoff, creates project plan | Defines problem statement and success metrics | Provides technical feasibility input | Advises on team capacity and process | Conducts initial user research | Defines baseline metrics | Shares customer feedback and pain points |
| **Planning** | Manages timeline and resources | Prioritizes backlog items | Estimates and plans work | Facilitates sprint planning | Creates wireframes and prototypes | Sets up tracking dashboards | Provides customer requirements input |
| **Execution** | Tracks progress, manages risks | Clarifies requirements, reviews deliverables | Builds and tests features | Facilitates ceremonies, removes blockers | Iterates on designs, supports implementation | Monitors metrics, provides insights | Gathers ongoing customer feedback |
| **Release** | Coordinates deployment schedule | Validates acceptance criteria | Deploys and verifies | Supports release process | Validates UX in production | Tracks launch metrics | Communicates to customers, monitors adoption |
| **Retrospective** | Facilitates or participates in retro | Provides product perspective | Shares technical learnings | Leads retrospective session | Shares design learnings | Presents outcome data | Shares customer impact feedback |

## Role Clarification Checklist

Use this checklist at project kickoff to ensure all essential roles are identified and responsibilities are clear.

- [ ] All required roles identified for this project
- [ ] Each role has a named owner assigned
- [ ] Responsibilities for each role reviewed and agreed upon
- [ ] Interaction points between roles clarified
- [ ] Communication cadence established for each role
- [ ] Escalation paths defined for each role
- [ ] Role-specific success criteria discussed
- [ ] Any role gaps or shared responsibilities documented

> **Tip:** Review this checklist during the first planning session and revisit during retrospectives.
